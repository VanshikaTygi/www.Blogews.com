    In Short
    Rodri wins Ballon d'Or, first for Man City.
    Rodri beat Vinicius Jr. and Jude Bellingham to win the title
    The midfielder was instrumental in Spain's Euro 2024 win
    Spain's Rodri became the first Manchester City footballer to win the Ballon d'Or on Monday, October 28. In a glitzy ceremony in Paris, Rodri beat Real Madrid duo of Vinicius Jr. and Jude Bellingham to clinch his maiden award. The Premier League and Euro 2024 champion became the first mid-fielder since Luka Modric to win the Ballon d'Or title.

    Rodri, had a phenomenal 2023/24 season that cemented his status as one of the world's top midfielders and potentially paved the way for him to win the prestigious Ballon d'Or. At Manchester City, Rodri was a pivotal figure in the team's dominant campaign. He played a crucial role in City's record-breaking fourth consecutive Premier League title. Rodri's statistical output was impressive; he scored 8 goals and provided 9 assists in the Premier League.
    Rodri's success was not limited to his club performances; he was also instrumental in Spain's triumph at Euro 2024. He played in all 11 competitive matches for Spain during the tournament and was a key figure in their unbeaten run leading up to the final. Despite suffering an injury that forced him to leave the game at halftime in the final against England, Rodri's overall impact on the tournament was significant. He scored a crucial equalizer against Georgia in the Round of 16, demonstrating his ability to perform under pressure.

  • News :- Ranji Trophy 2024/25: Madhav Kaushik's Emphatic Ton Keeps UP In Commanding Position Vs Punjab

    • Blog :-
    Ranji Trophy 2024/25: Madhav Kaushik's ton gives UP an 83-run lead vs Punjab at Stumps on Day 2. Read below for more.
    By : PTI | Updated at : 27 Oct 2024 11:05 PM (IST)

    Ranji Trophy 2024/25: Opening batter Madhav Kaushik's unbeaten 128 handed Uttar Pradesh an upper hand against Punjab as they reached 293 for 3 on Day 2 of their Ranji Trophy Group C match here on Sunday.

    UP resumed the day at 8/0 after the hosts made 210 in their first innings, and they are ahead by 83 runs at stumps with seven wickets in hand.

    After 34 runs were added in the day, Punjab got the breakthrough as UP skipper Aryan Juyal (16) fell to Gurnoor Brar.

    The Kaushik and Priyam Garg (27) pair added 43 more to the UP total before the latter became Brar's second victim. Although UP were trailing behind by five runs then, the Kaushik-Rinku Singh (46 not out off 85 balls) duo has so far shared a 88-run stand for the unconquered fourth wicket to keep UP in a comfortable position.
    Kaushik has so far hit 17 fours during his 231-ball unbeaten innings. On Saturday, Punjab were bowled out for 210, courtesy of Shivam Mavi's four-wicket haul, while Pukhraj Mann (61) and Sanvir Singh (50) were the top scorers for the hosts.

  • News :- Novak Djokovic beats Rafael Nadal in 'One Last Dance' at Six Kings Slam

    • Blog :-
    Six Kings Slam: Novak Djokovic defeated Rafael Nadal in straight sets in the third-place playoff-match on Saturday. Djokovic won the match 6-2, 7-6 (7-5). The duo faced each other on the tour 60 times since 2006 but won't be meeting anymore as Nadal will retire from professional tennis after the Davis Cup tie against Netherlands.

    In Short
    Novak Djokovic beat Rafael Nadal 6-2, 7-6 (7-5) on Saturday
    Djokovic and Nadal faced each other 60 times on the tour
    Nadal will retire later this year after the Davis Cup tie against the Netherlands
    Novak Djokovic beat Rafael Nadal in straight sets in One Last Dance between the legends of the game in the inaugural edition of the Six Kings Slam in Riyadh. On Saturday, October 19, Djokovic beat Nadal 6-2, 7-6 (7-5) in the third-place playoff match in front of a packed house.

    Nadal and Djokovic have shared a great rivalry over the years, with the Serb leading the Spaniard 31-29 on the ATP tour. Djokovic had earlier beaten Nadal in straight sets in the Paris Olympics two months ago. The two won’t be meeting any more on the tour as Nadal will retire from professional tennis after the Davis Cup tie against the Netherlands later this year.

    “The rivalry has been incredible. It’s been very intense. I hope we will have a chance to sit on the beach somewhere and have a drink reflecting on life and talking about something else,” Djokovic said in the on-court interview after the match.
