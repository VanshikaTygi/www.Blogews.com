Are you reaching your full potential? A guide to personal development


What is personal development?

Personal development is looking inward and focusing on ways to better yourself. Personal development increases your self-awareness, your self-esteem, increases your skills, and fulfills your aspirations.

At BetterUp, we’re big believers in the practice of Inner Work®. In many ways, personal development is a form of Inner Work®. It is the act of looking inward to achieve a purpose or result, especially in the pursuit of clarity, purpose, and passion in life.

Personal development is much larger than just career development or self-development. It encompasses all aspects of your life where you’d like to see personal growth — and it doesn’t discriminate on where it shows up for you.

5 areas of personal development:-

Mental

Exercising your brain and staying mentally fit is critical to personal development.
Mental development can be anything from workshops, training sessions, or even just rest. Rest is just as (if not more) important than mental exercise. And as your mental fitness improves, so does your resiliency, innovation, and self-awareness.

Social

Social connections and relationships are also a must-have for any personal development. After all, self-improvement is not a solitary pursuit. Humans are social creatures — we need that connection to learn and grow.
And from social connections, we gain important skills. Like communication skills, problem-solving, relationship-building, and the ability to receive (and give) feedback.

Spiritual

Personal growth and development — whether you’re religious or not — can come through in spirituality. It’s important to recognize that religion and spirituality are not synonymous. In fact, far from it.
Spirituality means you’re investing in a deeper understanding of your own self in the world around you. It helps you get to know yourself and uncover your values.

Emotional

Emotional personal development often goes hand-in-hand with emotional intelligence. At its simplest, emotional intelligence is the ability to understand feelings. Beyond feelings, it’s understanding how those feelings shape our thoughts and actions. If you’re looking back at your journey and identifying the growth, it’s likely you’ve fostered a sense of emotional development along the way. This requires emotional intelligence to understand what role your feelings and thoughts played.

Physical

A healthy body nourishes a healthy mind. There’s an intrinsic link between your physical, mental, and emotional well-being.
This aspect of personal development is focused on physical health — like nutrition, sleep, exercise, and movement. When your physical self is nourished, it’s likely your mind is also reaping those benefits.

The benefits of personal development

Increased self-awareness and self-esteem.
Increased mental fitness.
Greater resilience.
More knowledge and learned skills.
Improved personal and professional relationships.

6 steps to developing a personal development plan

1. Identify areas where you’d like to improve
2. Work with a coach or mentor to assemble a plan
3. Structure your personal development goals
4. Find an accountability buddy
5. Be honest and aware of your progress
6. Factor personal development into your mental fitness plan

How personalized development can help

Personal development is just that: it’s personal.
Every human is different. If our society cracked the nut on personal development, every self-help book would help everyone. Or every learning process would benefit every student.
But we know from our life experiences that there’s no “one-size-fits-all” solution to anything when it comes to human nature. Push yourself outside of your comfort zone. By doing so, you'll be on the path to reaching your full potential.
While self-actualization and Inner Work® is a lifelong process, your personal development goals shouldn't be daunting. Start small — like building a new skill or even investing in work-life balance. By doing so, you'll set yourself up for success in all areas of your life.
If you're ready to make a change to improve the quality of your life, consider how personal and professional development coaching can help.



The Ultimate Guide on Time Management

Time management is the ability to plan and organize the time you have effectively so that you know how much time to devote to each task, activity, or project.
High achievers know how to manage time properly, as such, they unlock the many benefits of effective time management, including:
Greater clarity and capability
Greater efficiency and productivity
Greater focus and performance
Less procrastination
Reduced stress
More opportunities to achieve personal and professional goals
Better work-life balance
Better prioritization
Better scheduling
Nevertheless, time management is easier said than done. As with any skill, it takes lots of practice to perfect it.

Why Is Time Management Important?

Not only will good time management stop you from wasting time on unimportant tasks, it will enable you to focus on the things that really matter to your business and lifestyle.
With effective time management practices, you can work more efficiently, improve your productivity, reduce stress, and achieve a better work-life balance.

The Basic Principle of Time Management – Stephen Covey’s Matrix

If you aren’t familiar with Stephen Covey’s Time Management Matrix, it’s a popular tool for those interested in proper time management and prioritization. The matrix is divided into four quadrants:
Urgent and important
Not urgent but important
Urgent but not important
Not urgent not important
Each quadrant represents a different level of urgency and importance.

This time management technique enables you to identify and analyze tasks, and then categorize them in order of which one’s most important. By doing so, you can schedule your workdays properly and prioritize time to accomplish the tasks accordingly.

Time Anxiety: How to Handle It and Boost Productivity

Time anxiety is the fear of failing to manage one’s daily tasks and responsibilities effectively. People suffering from time anxiety have this persistent feeling that they will never accomplish what they want. They are always racing against the clock, struggling to beat deadlines and keep up with their responsibilities.
Fortunately, there are effective time management strategies that can help you overcome time anxiety, including time blocking, prioritization, mindfulness, and structuring your workdays.

How to Improve Time Management

Most people fail to accomplish their daily, weekly, or monthly targets and goals due to poor time management, failure to plan, and a lack of organizational skills. The thing is, effective time management takes practice and discipline.
Fortunately, anyone can learn effective time management and avoid falling into the planning fallacy. By setting reminders, creating a to-do list, working with a daily planner, prioritizing, breaking big projects into smaller tasks, and adopting a growth mindset, you can achieve more in less time.

The 5-Step Time Management Plan

Write down your short term and long term goals
Determine what your time is worth.
Keep a time Log for one at least one week
Analyze your time log to identify the time-wasting activities (ie, activities that don’t help you reach your long and short term goals)
Set up a plan to eliminate the time wasters in your day.

Time Management Apps

We live in a fast-paced digital world where digital time management tools have become necessary for anyone, whether you work from home or the office, looking to stay organized and productive.
These tools offer features like calendar integrations, reports, timesheets, reminders, and task lists to enable you to stay focused and on top of your schedule.



The Importance of Mental Health Awareness


Mental illnesses affect 19% of the adult population, 46% of teenagers and 13% of children each year. People struggling with their mental health may be in your family, live next door, teach your children, work in the next cubicle or sit in the same church pew.
However, only half of those affected receive treatment, often because of the stigma attached to mental health.

What Exactly is a Mental Illness

A mental illness is a physical illness of the brain that causes disturbances in thinking, behavior, energy or emotion that make it difficult to cope with the ordinary demands of life. Research is starting to uncover the complicated causes of these diseases which can include genetics, brain chemistry, brain structure, experiencing trauma and/or having another medical condition, like heart disease.
The two most common mental health conditions are:

Anxiety Disorders

More than 19% of adults each year struggle with some type of anxiety disorder, including: post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), panic disorder (panic attacks), generalized anxiety disorder and specific phobias.

Mood Disorders

Mood disorders, such as depression and bipolar depression, affect nearly 10% of adults each year and are characterized by difficulties in regulating one’s mood.

What You Can Do to Help


Although the general perception of mental illness has improved over the past decades, studies show that stigma against mental illness is still powerful, largely due to media stereotypes and lack of education, and that people tend to attach negative stigmas to mental health conditions at a far higher rate than to other diseases and disabilities, such as cancer, diabetes or heart disease.
Stigma and misinformation can feel like overwhelming obstacles for someone who is struggling with a mental health condition. Here a few powerful things you can do to help:
Showing individuals respect and acceptance removes a significant barrier to successfully coping with their illness. Having people see you as an individual and not as your illness can make the biggest difference for someone who is struggling with their mental health.
Advocating within our circles of influence helps ensure these individuals have the same rights and opportunities as other members of your church, school and community.
Learning more about mental health allows us to provide helpful support to those affected in our families and communities.
